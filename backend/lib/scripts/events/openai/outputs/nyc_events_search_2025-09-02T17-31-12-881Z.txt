Here are several truly notable events that took place in New York City between July 2 and September 2, 2025—events that would have drawn both tourists and locals, and garnered significant media interest. I’ve focused only on those of real impact and prominence.

---

**1. Macy’s 4th of July Fireworks & Nathan’s Hot Dog Eating Contest (July 4)**  
This annual Independence Day tradition remains a magnet for crowds and media coverage alike. The East River skyline lights up with Macy’s breathtaking fireworks, while the Nathan’s Famous Hot Dog Eating Contest in Coney Island continues its quirky, headline-grabbing appeal.([overherenewyork.com](https://www.overherenewyork.com/new-york-city-events-in-july-2025/?utm_source=openai), [nyctourism.com](https://www.nyctourism.com/annual-events?utm_source=openai), [gothambuzz.nyc](https://www.gothambuzz.nyc/article/578/things-to-do-in-nyc-july-2025-july-events-nyc-2025?utm_source=openai))

---

**2. SummerStage & Shakespeare in the Park (July–August)**  
New Yorkers and visitors alike flock to enjoy free, high-quality cultural programming:
- **SummerStage** spans parks across all five boroughs, offering diverse musical performances—from jazz and indie to hip-hop—throughout July and August.([rcourihay.com](https://rcourihay.com/blog/the-best-new-york-city-summer-2025-happenings/?utm_source=openai), [nyctourism.com](https://www.nyctourism.com/annual-events?utm_source=openai))  
- **Shakespeare in the Park**, performed in Central Park's Delacorte Theatre, reopened this summer after renovations. This free-from-audience-production of *Othello* (July) and *Twelfth Night* (August onward) attracts huge crowds and enduring affection.([overherenewyork.com](https://www.overherenewyork.com/new-york-city-events-in-july-2025/?utm_source=openai), [rcourihay.com](https://rcourihay.com/blog/the-best-new-york-city-summer-2025-happenings/?utm_source=openai), [nyctourism.com](https://www.nyctourism.com/annual-events?utm_source=openai))

---

**3. “Summer Streets” Citywide Car-Free Festival (Late July–August)**  
In honor of NYC’s 400th anniversary, *Summer Streets* was expanded to cover over 400 blocks across all five boroughs. Held on five consecutive Saturdays from July 26 through August 23, it turned major thoroughfares into vibrant pedestrian zones filled with performance, art installations, fitness classes, and pop-up entertainment.([secretnyc.co](https://secretnyc.co/things-to-do-in-august-nyc-events-2025/?utm_source=openai), [timeout.com](https://www.timeout.com/newyork/events-calendar/august-events-calendar?utm_source=openai))

---

**4. Jazz Age Lawn Party on Governors Island (August 9–10)**  
This beloved, immersive event celebrating the Roaring ’20s was back for its 20th anniversary. Featuring live jazz, vintage dancing, period costumes, cocktails, and vintage cars, it transformed Governors Island into a glamorous Gatsby-style fête.([secretnyc.co](https://secretnyc.co/things-to-do-in-august-nyc-events-2025/?utm_source=openai))

---

**5. Van Gogh "Starry Nights" at the New York Botanical Garden (August into October)**  
This immersive exhibition combined art and nature in a spectacular way. Visitors enjoyed Van Gogh’s floral works by day, followed by an illuminated drone show inspired by *Starry Night* at dusk—an enchanting crossover of artistry and technology.([secretnyc.co](https://secretnyc.co/things-to-do-in-august-nyc-events-2025/?utm_source=openai))

---

**6. Cultural Festivals in Harlem, Queens, and Beyond (August)**  
August featured several large-scale cultural gatherings:
- **Harlem Week** culminated mid-August in a week-long celebration of music, community, youth activities, and soul food.([new-york-tickets.com](https://www.new-york-tickets.com/travel-guide/best-time-to-visit/new-york-in-august/?utm_source=openai), [nyctourism.com](https://www.nyctourism.com/annual-events?utm_source=openai))  
- The **Hong Kong Dragon Boat Festival** returned August 9–10 at Flushing Meadows, featuring a dramatic, well-attended rowing spectacle.([new-york-tickets.com](https://www.new-york-tickets.com/travel-guide/best-time-to-visit/new-york-in-august/?utm_source=openai))  
- The **Dominican Day Parade** brightened Sixth Avenue on August 10 with vivid floats, music, and Dominican flair.([new-york-tickets.com](https://www.new-york-tickets.com/travel-guide/best-time-to-visit/new-york-in-august/?utm_source=openai))  
- **Afropunk Festival**, spanning genres and fashion, also took place late August in Brooklyn’s Commodore Barry Park.([new-york-tickets.com](https://www.new-york-tickets.com/travel-guide/best-time-to-visit/new-york-in-august/?utm_source=openai))

**7. US Open Tennis (August 24 – ongoing through early September)**  
This Grand Slam event dominates NYC’s sporting calendar. Beginning August 24 at Flushing Meadows, the US Open draws international tennis stars and massive fan turnout, extending into early September.([us.trip.com](https://us.trip.com/events/4938655-2025-08-new-york-collection/?utm_source=openai), [nyctourism.com](https://www.nyctourism.com/annual-events?utm_source=openai), [trip.com](https://www.trip.com/events/5767721-2025-09-new-york-collection/?utm_source=openai))

**8. Lady Gaga’s Madison Square Garden Residency (August 22–September 7)**  
Lady Gaga's ‘Mayhem Ball Tour’ rocked MSG across six spectacular nights, starting August 22. With dramatic theatrical production and a wide-ranging setlist—including “Poker Face” and new material—this was one of the major music highlights of late summer.([nypost.com](https://nypost.com/2025/08/08/ticket-sales/lady-gaga-madison-square-garden-2025-where-to-buy-tickets-best-prices/?utm_source=openai))

**9. Climate Week NYC (late August/early September, aligning with UN General Assembly)**  
Climate Week 2025 convened global leaders, activists, and businesses to tackle pressing climate challenges—amid rising political headwinds. Held alongside the UN General Assembly, it hosted around 900 events focused on decarbonization, clean energy, and climate solutions.([reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/climate-energy/why-climate-week-new-york-matters-this-year-more-than-ever-2025-08-27/?utm_source=openai))

**10. West Indian American Day Parade (Labor Day Weekend)**  
This vibrant celebration of Caribbean culture unfolded along Eastern Parkway in Brooklyn, featuring music, costumes, and political presence—mayoral candidates made gestures to the crowds. Heightened security measures were in place following the previous year’s tragedy.([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/86bca6b74f405a7a05bfcdcf31a9eda3?utm_source=openai))
